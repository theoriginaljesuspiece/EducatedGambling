# **My Own Problems**

### Right bias, wrong entry
I would find myself with the correct directional bias BUT as soon as I enter the trade, I get stopped out by a few ticks or 1-2 points and then price heads to my target... DAFUQ!?
>__

### Invalid invalidation points
When I would place my stops at points of "invalidation", it actually isn't the case...
>__

### Patience
Sometimes I would not recognise that my initial trades come from the thought of "forcing" trades to work rather than letting it happen. The idea of "getting it done quickly" destroyed my patience.
>__

### Reacting to everything
I have a personality trait called 'sensory processing sentitivity' which pretty much makes my nervous system fire off at the smallest stimuli. I would react to majority of the sudden bids/offers that would enter the market and the shift it would cause.
>_Visually, remove all the uncessary things that you think you need but you don't i.e. the 4 other charts that you have open or the +5 indicators you have. Utilise the only ones that you need, all the rest are just noise._


### Prepared but unprepared
I think that simply reading what has happened in the market 10-20 minutes before trading is enough preparation. Because of this there would be things that happen which I wasn't prepared for i.e. news events, multiday or multiweek levels etc.
>_My preparation comprises of analysing supply and demand levels according to volume profile variances for the week (and maybe several others depending on the market condition), Another important aspect is **[Carmine Rosato's CLC rule](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7facFfjQ0UE)**. It is pretty much an analytical way of drawing contextual narrative of the market. It is hard to try to incorporate it while attempting discretional trading as a beginner (even for intermediates)._ 
>
>_How I would use it:_
>* _**Context** and **Location** should be in your premarket plan, or noted/charted/drawn before you trade so you can refer to it during the session_
>* _**Confirmation** is used during your trades_

### Trading multiple sessions

I live in Australia so I have the benefit of trading ETH sessions and the curse of trading RTH sessions. The problem is that when prop firming, the EOD is calculated in your local time so my wins or losses actually stack up which skews "consistency rules".
>_I have allocated different sessions their own prop accounts. Makes it easier to manage risk and consistency, and the accounts in general._
>
>_For example, Lucid accounts for NY session and Apex accounts for London session._
